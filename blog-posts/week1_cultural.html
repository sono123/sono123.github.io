DBC After A Week

By Steve Ono, Posted on June 8, 2014

Kitchen vs. Table

After a week 1 of Phase 0, I can say that there were many surprises that I didnt expect. The experience was definitely a positive one though. I'll start with the negative first, just so we can get that out of the way.

My only complaints would revolve around the necessary time to commit to this program. I didnt realize that there would be this amount of work during Phase 0. I totally acknowledge that I may have simply not noticed this information when reading about the course, but I do think it wasnt spelled out clear enough. I work around 60 hours a week at my family business. Although I have flexibility, this makes it very difficult to to find the additional 15-25 hours per week.

As for the positives, I love the Kitchen vs. Table analogy that Sherif made. I am definitely the type of person who likes to push the boundaries and change things. I like to extract the most that I can out of many situations. I love positive energy, and hard working people. It was such a relief to hear that this type of attitude is encouraged at Dev Bootcamp. I've definitly had thoughts ranging from "Will this program be good enough?" to "Am I good enough". So, it was nice to hear that other people have had a similar internal dialog. The truth is, I really didnt know what to expect going forward, but I knew that I am willing to bust my ass. After hearing Sherif speak about how this program works, I feel much more confident that I will get everything and some out of this experience.
